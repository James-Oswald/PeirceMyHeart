About Alpha Existential Graphs

What are Existential Graphs?

Proposed by Charles Sanders Peirce, an Existential Graph is a diagrammatic or visual representation of a logical expression. There are 3 types of Existential Graphs: Alpha Existential Graphs, Beta Existential Graphs and Gamma Existential Graphs. In our application, we cover Alpha Existential Graphs.

The parts of an Alpha Existential Graph (AEG)

Alpha Existential Graphs are a form of Existential Graphs that are isomorphic to Propositional Logic Propositional Logic. Graphs of this system can be used to construct entire propositional logic statements with just 3 components:
Sheet of Assertion
The blank page on which the graph is constructed.
Atoms
The letters or phrases which are used to represent the propositions.
Cuts
Fully closed curves that are used to represent negation. Everything contained within the cut will be considered to be negated. Cuts can be nested and concatenated with other cuts. Empty cuts, i.e. cuts that do not contain anything within them, are allowed to be present on the graph. Each cut represents a "level" in the graph; a nested cut represents one level deeper than the level of its parent cut.

How AEGs Work

Anything drawn on this sheet is asserted to be True. Phrases, subgraphs and even the entire graph can be equated to True or False.
Since everything drawn on the sheet is considered True, placing multiple atoms on the same levels can be considered as a conjunction between the atoms. This further applies to atoms and cuts on the same level as well (Note: Asserting that multiple propositions are True all at the same time in turn holds that the conjunction of those propositions is True as well).
Everything contained within a cut is considered negated. As such, an empty cut represents False.
An AEG introduces functionality to represent AND and NOT as seen in propositional logic. Furthermore, all other propositional connectives can be represented in an AEG just by using AND and NOT. For instance,
A OR B
can be represented as
NOT( NOTA AND (NOTB))
In an AEG, this would be represented by first drawing a CUT on the Sheet of Assertion. The, we would draw 2 smaller CUTs within the first CUT, and draw A within one and B within the other.
