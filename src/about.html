About Alpha Existential Graphs

What are Existential Graphs?

Proposed by Charles Sanders Peirce, an Existential Graph is a visual, also called diagrammatic, representation of some logical expression. There are three types of Existential Graphs: Alpha, Beta and Gamma. In Peirce My Heart, we have implemented only Alpha Existential Graphs (AEGs).

The components of an Alpha Existential Graph

AEGs are isomorphic to Propositional Logic. This means, simply, that all conclusions reachable through Propositional Logic are also reachable through AEGs. These following three components make up all AEGs:
The Sheet of Assertion
Blank page on which the graph is constructed. May be referred to as "The Sheet."
Atoms
Letters or phrases used to represent propositions. Peirce My Heart only supports letters.
Cuts
Fully closed curves representing negations. Every atom and cut contained within some other cut is considered negated. Cuts can be nested within other cuts. Empty cuts, i.e. cuts that do not contain anything, are allowed to exist on The Sheet. Each cut corresponds to an integer cut "level" in the graph, and a nested cut corresponds to one cut level deeper than the level of its parent.

How AEGs Work

Anything drawn on The Sheet is asserted as True. Phrases, subgraphs and The Sheet itself may evaluate to True or False.
Since everything drawn on The Sheet is True, placing multiple atoms on the same level may be considered creating a conjunction between these atoms. This further applies to atoms and cuts on the same level (Note: Asserting that multiple propositions are True all at the same time means that the conjunction of these propositions is True as well).
Because everything contained within a cut is negated, and The Sheet corresponds to True, an empty cut corresponds to False.
AEGs contain functionality to represent AND and NOT, as seen in propositional logic. Further, all propositional connectives can be represented in an AEG by using some combination of AND and NOT. For instance,
A OR B
can be represented as
NOT(NOTA AND (NOTB))
In an AEG, this would be represented by first drawing a cut on the Sheet of Assertion. The, we would draw 2 smaller cuts within the first cut, and draw A within one and B within the other.
